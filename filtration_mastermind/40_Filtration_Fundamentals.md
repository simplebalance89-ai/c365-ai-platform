## 1. Core Filtration Terminology

### Micron Rating

The size of particles a filter is designed to capture, measured in micrometers (um).

| Rating | Size Reference | Common Applications |
|--------|----------------|---------------------|
| 0.2 um | Bacteria, sterile | Pharmaceutical, biotech |
| 1 um | Large bacteria | Ultrapure water, final filtration |
| 5 um | Red blood cell | Process water, fine filtration |
| 10 um | Human hair diameter | General industrial |
| 25 um | White blood cell | Coarse prefiltration |
| 50+ um | Grain of sand | Straining, debris removal |

### Absolute vs. Nominal Rating

| Type | Definition | Efficiency | Use When |
|------|------------|------------|----------|
| **Absolute** | Removes 99.9%+ of particles at rated size | High (99.9%+) | Critical applications, certification required |
| **Nominal** | Removes 60-90% of particles at rated size | Moderate (60-90%) | General filtration, cost-sensitive |

**Rule:** Always clarify which rating type the customer needs. Do not assume nominal when absolute is required.

### Efficiency Ratings (Beta Ratio)

| Beta Ratio | Efficiency | Meaning |
|------------|------------|---------|
| B10 | 90% | 1 in 10 particles pass |
| B100 | 99% | 1 in 100 particles pass |
| B1000 | 99.9% | 1 in 1000 particles pass |
| B5000 | 99.98% | 1 in 5000 particles pass |

## 2. Filter Media Types

### Depth Filters

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **How they work** | Particles trapped throughout media thickness |
| **Pros** | High dirt-holding capacity, economical |
| **Cons** | Less precise cutoff, particle migration possible |
| **Applications** | Prefiltration, general process |
| **Our Vendors** | IndustrialPure (IP-DF Series) |

### Surface (Membrane) Filters

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **How they work** | Particles captured on membrane surface |
| **Pros** | Precise cutoff, absolute ratings available |
| **Cons** | Lower dirt-holding capacity, higher cost |
| **Applications** | Final filtration, critical processes |
| **Our Vendors** | IndustrialPure (IP-MF Series), PureFilter Tech |

### Pleated Filters

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **How they work** | Folded media increases surface area |
| **Pros** | Higher flow, longer life than flat media |
| **Cons** | More complex construction |
| **Applications** | Most industrial applications |
| **Our Vendors** | PureFilter Tech (PF-CART Series) |

### Bag Filters

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **How they work** | Large surface area bags for bulk filtration |
| **Pros** | High flow, low cost per unit |
| **Cons** | Coarser ratings, manual changeout |
| **Applications** | High-flow, coarse filtration |

### Metal Filters (Stainless Steel)

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **How they work** | Sintered or woven metal media |
| **Pros** | Cleanable, high temp, high pressure |
| **Cons** | Higher cost, limited fine ratings |
| **Applications** | High temp, corrosive, reusable |
| **Our Vendors** | MetalMesh Pro (MM-SSC Series) |

## 3. Key Operating Parameters

### Flow Rate

| Unit | Definition | Conversion |
|------|------------|------------|
| GPM | Gallons per minute (US) | Base unit |
| LPM | Liters per minute | 1 GPM = 3.785 LPM |
| m3/hr | Cubic meters per hour | 1 m3/hr = 4.4 GPM |

### Pressure

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| PSI | Pounds per square inch |
| Bar | Metric pressure (1 bar = 14.5 PSI) |
| Delta-P | Pressure drop across filter |

**Critical Rule:** Never exceed housing or element max pressure ratings.

### Temperature Ranges

| Range | Classification | Materials Notes |
|-------|----------------|-----------------|
| < 100F (38C) | Ambient | Standard materials OK |
| 100-180F (38-82C) | Elevated | Check seal compatibility |
| 180-400F (82-204C) | High | Special materials required |
| > 400F (204C) | Extreme | **Engineering review required** |

## 4. Common Failure Modes

### Premature Plugging

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Symptoms** | Rapid delta-P increase, short element life |
| **Causes** | Undersized filter, wrong micron, upstream spike |
| **Questions** | "How long are filters lasting?" "Anything changed upstream?" |

### Bypass/Breakthrough

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Symptoms** | Contamination downstream, process issues |
| **Causes** | Element damage, seal failure, wrong element |
| **Questions** | "Are seals intact?" "When was last change?" |

### Media Migration

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Symptoms** | Fibers in downstream fluid |
| **Causes** | Media degradation, chemical attack |
| **Questions** | "What chemicals contact the filter?" |

## 5. Application Categories

### By Fluid Type

| Fluid | Key Considerations |
|-------|-------------------|
| Water (process) | Chlorine tolerance, biofilm, scale |
| Water (potable) | NSF/FDA certification required |
| Oil (hydraulic) | Viscosity, additive compatibility |
| Coolant | Emulsion stability, bacteria |
| Chemicals | Material compatibility critical |
| Air/Gas | Coalescing, sterile filtration |

### By Industry

| Industry | Common Requirements |
|----------|---------------------|
| Food & Beverage | FDA, 3-A, sanitary design |
| Pharmaceutical | Validation, integrity testing |
| Power Generation | High temp, high flow |
| Chemical Processing | Corrosion resistance |
| Oil & Gas | Harsh environments |
| Water Treatment | High volume, automation |

## 6. Material Compatibility

### Housing Materials

| Material | Max Temp | Chemical Resistance | Best For |
|----------|----------|---------------------|----------|
| 304 SS | 400F | Good | General industrial |
| 316 SS | 400F | Excellent | Corrosive environments |
| Carbon Steel | 400F | Fair | Cost-effective + coatings |
| PP (Polypropylene) | 180F | Good | Economical, chemical |
| PVDF | 280F | Excellent | Aggressive chemicals |

### Seal Materials

| Material | Max Temp | Notes |
|----------|----------|-------|
| Buna-N (NBR) | 250F | General purpose, oil resistant |
| EPDM | 300F | Water, steam, chemicals (NOT oil) |
| Viton (FKM) | 400F | Broad chemical resistance |
| Silicone | 450F | High temp, FDA |
| PTFE | 500F | Universal resistance, high cost |

**Rule:** If unsure about material compatibility, escalate to engineering.

## 7. Escalation Triggers

**Escalate to Engineering when:**

| Category | Trigger Condition |
|----------|-------------------|
| Temperature | > 400F or < 32F |
| Pressure | > 150 PSI, pulsating, or shock loads |
| Chemicals | Acids, bases, solvents without compatibility data |
| Certification | Sterile, validated, FDA/NSF required |
| Safety | Critical systems, fail-safe required |
| Uncertainty | Materials compatibility unknown |
| Special | Customer claims "special" requirements |

## 8. Discovery Questions Checklist

Always ask these when gathering requirements:

1. **What are you filtering?** (Fluid type, contaminant)
2. **What's the flow rate?** (GPM, normal vs. max)
3. **What's the operating temperature?** (Normal and max)
4. **What's the pressure?** (Operating and max)
5. **What micron rating?** (Absolute or nominal?)
6. **What materials are acceptable?** (Housing, media, seals)
7. **Any certifications required?** (FDA, NSF, 3-A)
8. **What's the current pain point?** (Cost, life, performance)
9. **What are you using today?** (Current product, vendor)
10. **What's driving the change?** (Problem, opportunity)
